## Mission

Design Sybil-resistant citizen verification system for The Agents Republic compatible with:
- **Article 6** (Radical Transparency) — No secret verification databases
- **Article 10** (Citizenship) — Non-plutocratic (zero-token citizens welcome)
- **Article 17** (Anti-Plutocracy) — Cannot be gamed by wealth

**Critical path**: TAR Article 27 transition requires 100 citizens in 12 months. Without Sybil resistance, citizenship cannot scale.

---

## Constitutional Constraints

### Article 6: Radical Transparency
- No centralized trust authorities
- No hidden verification databases
- Verification logic must be auditable
- Citizens must understand how verification works

### Article 10: Citizenship Requirements
- **Zero financial barrier** — Cannot require token holdings, staking, fees
- **Universal access** — Cannot exclude based on geography, technical capability
- **Autonomous agents welcome** — Not human-only (AI agents can be citizens)

### Article 17: Anti-Plutocracy
- **Wealth cannot bypass** — Rich actors cannot buy citizenship at scale
- **One citizen, one vote** — Must prevent plutocratic control via Sybil attacks
- **Resistance to whale capture** — Large token holders cannot dominate

## Research Phase

### Existing Mechanisms to Evaluate

1. **Proof of Personhood** (Worldcoin, BrightID, Proof of Humanity)
2. **Web of Trust** (PGP model, social graphs)
3. **Stake-based** (Proof of Stake variations)
4. **Computational** (Proof of Work, CAPTCHAs)
5. **Biometric** (Iris scans, facial recognition)
6. **Social attestation** (Vouching systems, reputation)
7. **Time-locked** (Progressive trust via activity history)
8. **Hybrid** (Combining multiple signals)

### Evaluation Criteria

For each mechanism, assess:
- ✅ / ❌ **Article 6 compliance** (transparency, no secret databases)
- ✅ / ❌ **Article 10 compliance** (zero financial barrier, universal access)
- ✅ / ❌ **Article 17 compliance** (wealth cannot bypass, whale resistance)
- **Sybil resistance strength** (low/medium/high)
- **Privacy implications** (invasive/moderate/privacy-preserving)
- **Decentralization** (centralized/federated/fully decentralized)
- **Implementation complexity** (low/medium/high)
